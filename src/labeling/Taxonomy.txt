* not climate opinion - This describes posts that are not about climate change. Rule: use if climate is not the topic of discussion.
* climate activism - Posts that urge people to help with climate change. This can include asking how individuals can help with climate change, or promoting projects centered around climate change or its effects. Rule: Use if it urges someone to pursue action.
* climate anxiety - A post that shows a fear of climate change, without necessarily being disheartened about beating it. Rule: Use if they express fear/distress, but not hopelessness/resignation.
* climate change importance - Posts that express the importance of climate change. This might not directly equate to activism, as expressing importance does not necessarily equate to urging action. Rule: the text expresses the continent "pay attention to this", not "do this action"
* climate apathy - The author of the post does not care about climate change. Rule: use if the text expresses disinterest, but not hopelessness.
* climate information - An informational post about climate change. This could include quoting a news article without adding further opinions, or mentioning a natural disaster. 
* climate nihilism - This is the opinion that climate change is inevitable and irreversible. This surpasses climate anxiety, in that the author is hopeless in stopping climate change. Climate nihilism includes the belief that any human action to help the climate is futile, resulting in feelings of hopelessness and disengagement. Rule: the text includes one or more of the sentiment: Humans are destined for catastrophe, and action is either impossible or insufficient; Climate catastrophe has been years in the making, and the damage has already been dealt; Dismissing past actions that have been taken to slow climate change.
* climate denial - The opinion that climate change does not exist. Rule: use if the text expresses sentiments denying the existence of climate change.
* climate optimism - Expressing the idea that climate change is improving, or expressing hope/approval about current actions taken to slow climate change. This could also express hope that leaders or individuals can improve the climate. Rule: use if the text expresses a positive sentiment towards solutions or action.
* climate policy critique - This is a critique of political policy related to climate change. This includes how policies (or lack of policies) are hurting the climate, or if politicians are hurting climate change through their direct actions or inactions. Rule: this is a focus on institution or policy.
* climate action critique - A critique on climate change action. This includes critiquing an action plan, actions taken, or intentions towards climate change. This could include a company's actions towards climate change, AI data centers, etc. Rule: critiques outside information relating to climate action or inaction, not laws. 
* climate denial critique - A critique on those that deny climate change exists. Rule: focus on denial sentiments.
* climate nihilism critique - A critique on expressions of hopeless and fatalist views on climate change. Rule: focus on text that rejects nihilistic sentiments.
* climate opinion critique A critique on other opinions that do not fit into the above topics. Rule: use when all other sentiments are unclear.
